Md Saiful Hoque, Ph.D.

President of the Institute of Textile Science (ITS) | Innovating at the Intersection of Materials Science, Healthcare, and Textile Engineering

I am a materials scientist and textile engineer with over a decade of experience spanning academic research, industrial manufacturing, and higher education. Currently serving as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the University of Toronto's Institute of Biomedical Engineering and the KITE Research Institute (UHN), my mission is to design intelligent, user-centered wearable technologies that improve patient outcomes.

My career is built on a unique triad of expertise: rigorous academic research, practical industrial application, and a deep commitment to scientific knowledge mobilization.

The Unique Blend

🔬

Research & Innovation

  • Healthcare Wearables: Leading multidisciplinary initiatives to develop e-textiles and wearable sensors for physiological monitoring, thermoregulation, and treating conditions like sleep apnea.
  • Funding & Discovery: Secured over $400,000 in competitive research grants. My doctoral research at the University of Alberta led to a breakthrough discovery identifying the root causes of premature aging in high-performance protective fabrics.
  • Global Collaboration: Fostering international research partnerships, including collaborative smart textile projects between Canada and France.
🏭

Industry & Commercialization

  • Manufacturing Expertise: Grounded in real-world manufacturing, having worked as a Research & Development Officer and Engineering Intern in the textile industry where I oversaw product development from concept to industrial scale-up.
  • Startup Mentorship: Actively advising health-tech startups on material selection, prototyping, and performance as a mentor at KITE Creates.
📚

Leadership & Education

  • Scientific Leadership: Currently serving as the President of the Institute of Textile Science (ITS) in Canada, driving national initiatives for textile innovation.
  • Academic Service: Theme Editor for the IEEE EMBC 2026 Conference, focusing on user-centered smart textiles (From Fashion to Function).
  • Teaching & Mentorship: Designed and delivered undergraduate courses in textile engineering and have mentored over 20 emerging junior researchers, ranging from high school students to doctoral candidates.
